# Legumes (Όσπρια)

Legumes are the backbone of Greek home cooking — nutritious, economical, and delicious.

## Essential Legumes
- **Fasolia (White Beans)**: Fasolada, gigantes
- **Fakes (Lentils)**: Lentil soup
- **Revithia (Chickpeas)**: Stews, salads
- **Koukia (Fava Beans)**: Fresh or dried
- **Fava (Split Peas)**: Santorini specialty

## Classic Preparations
- Fasolada — the national dish (bean soup)
- Gigantes plaki — giant beans in tomato
- Fakes — lentil soup with vinegar
- Fava — Santorini split pea purée
- Revithada — chickpea stew (Sifnos)

## Cooking Tips
- Soak dried beans overnight (except lentils)
- Add salt after cooking (prevents tough skins)
- Dried beans need 1-2 hours cooking
- Finish with olive oil and lemon
- Better the next day

## Fasting Food
Legumes are central to Greek Orthodox fasting — satisfying, protein-rich, and meat-free.
