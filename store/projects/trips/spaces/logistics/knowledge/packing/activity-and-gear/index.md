# Activity and gear

Beyond climate, the strongest signal for what to pack is the itinerary itself — a hiking day, a
beach day, a formal dinner, or a long transit leg each implies specific items that a
climate-only pass would miss entirely. The packer should read through the trip's `itinerary_items`
(and any relevant `transit_legs`) looking for these concrete triggers rather than relying on
climate alone.

`activity-to-gear-mapping.md` covers the recurring activity types and what each one specifically
requires — hiking, beach/water activities, a full day of city walking, and a formal or upscale
dinner. `documents-and-electronics.md` covers the essentials that aren't climate- or
activity-driven at all but are near-universal for any trip: passport copies, power adapters,
chargers, and offline maps/tickets.

As with climate-driven items, every activity-driven item should cite the specific itinerary item
or leg that triggered it in its `reason` — "hiking day in Sintra (itinerary: 'Peninha hike')"
justifies boots; a bare "gear" entry with no source does not.
