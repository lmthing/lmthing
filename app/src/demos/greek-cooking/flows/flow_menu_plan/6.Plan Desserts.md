# Plan Desserts

Greek desserts are rich and meant to be shared:

## Syrup-Soaked Sweets (Σιροπιαστά)
- **Baklava** — phyllo, nuts, honey syrup
- **Galaktoboureko** — custard in phyllo
- **Ravani/Revani** — semolina cake
- **Portokalopita** — orange phyllo cake
- **Kataifi** — shredded phyllo with nuts

## Cookies (Κουλουράκια)
- **Kourabiedes** — butter cookies with powdered sugar
- **Melomakarona** — honey-walnut cookies
- **Koulourakia** — twisted butter cookies
- **Paximadia** — twice-baked biscotti

## Spoon Sweets (Γλυκά του Κουταλιού)
Traditional preserved fruits served with coffee:
- Cherry, quince, fig, grape, orange
- Served in small portions with water

## Fresh Options
- **Greek yogurt** with honey and walnuts
- **Fresh fruit** — watermelon, figs, grapes
- **Karydopita** — walnut cake (less sweet)

## Holiday-Specific
- **Vasilopita** — New Year's cake with coin
- **Tsoureki** — Easter sweet bread
- **Diples** — fried honey pastry

## Serving Tips
- Serve with Greek coffee or espresso
- Desserts come AFTER clearing main course
- Smaller portions — these are rich
- Always offer fruit as lighter option
