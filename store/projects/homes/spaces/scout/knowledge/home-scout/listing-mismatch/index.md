# Finding contradictions within a listing

A `mismatch` finding is fundamentally different from a `photos` or `floorplan` finding: it's not
about reading one piece of evidence, it's about two pieces of the SAME listing disagreeing with each
other — a description's claim against a caption's detail, a stated amenity against a field that
implies otherwise, a price that doesn't square with everything else the listing says about itself.

`text-vs-evidence-contradictions.md` covers the common shapes these internal contradictions take;
`too-good-to-be-true.md` covers the specific, high-stakes case of a price or set of features that
looks implausibly good relative to the market the listing claims to be in — arguably the single most
valuable thing a mismatch check can catch, because it's the one a hurried house-hunter is most likely
to act on before noticing the catch themselves.
