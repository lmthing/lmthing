# Select Meze Course

Meze is essential — Greeks never serve main courses without appetizers first.

## Cold Meze (prepare ahead)
- **Tzatziki** — yogurt, cucumber, garlic
- **Taramosalata** — fish roe dip (pink)
- **Melitzanosalata** — smoky eggplant dip
- **Fava** — yellow split pea purée (Santorini)
- **Htipiti** — spicy feta dip
- **Dolmades** — stuffed grape leaves

## Hot Meze
- **Saganaki** — fried cheese (pan or shrimp version)
- **Kolokithokeftedes** — zucchini fritters
- **Spanakopita** — spinach pie (individual portions)
- **Keftedakia** — mini meatballs
- **Grilled halloumi** — with lemon

## Always Include
- **Feta** — simple block with oregano and olive oil
- **Olives** — Kalamata or throumbes
- **Bread** — crusty loaf for dipping

## Quantity Guide
- 2-3 cold meze per gathering
- 1-2 hot meze
- Plan for people to fill up on meze (they will)

## Timing
Serve meze immediately when guests arrive with ouzo, wine, or tsipouro.
