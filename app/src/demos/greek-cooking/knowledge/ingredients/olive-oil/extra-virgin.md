# Extra Virgin Olive Oil (Εξαιρετικό Παρθένο)

The gold standard for Greek cooking — use generously and proudly.

## Characteristics
- First cold pressing
- Acidity below 0.8%
- Distinct fruity/peppery notes
- Green to golden color

## Greek Varieties
- **Koroneiki** (Peloponnese): Most common, balanced
- **Kolovi** (Lesbos): Gentle, fruity
- **Adramytini** (Northern Greece): Aromatic
- **Tsounati** (Crete): Robust, peppery

## Best Uses
- Finishing dishes (drizzle to serve)
- Salad dressings
- Dips (tzatziki, fava)
- Low-heat sautéing
- Bread dipping

## Quality Indicators
- Harvest date on label (fresher = better)
- Dark bottle (protects from light)
- Peppery finish (polyphenols)
- Single origin preferred

## Greek Wisdom
"Λάδι στο λάδι" — oil upon oil. In Greek cooking, there's no such thing as too much good olive oil.
