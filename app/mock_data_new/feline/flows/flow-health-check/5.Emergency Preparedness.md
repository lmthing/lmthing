# Step 5: Emergency Preparedness

**Agent:** Vet Visit Planner

## Task

Create an emergency response plan and prepare necessary resources.

## Instructions

Compile emergency preparedness materials:

### 1. Emergency Contact Card

Create a wallet-sized card and post one by phone:

**Your Cat:** _________________ 
**Microchip #:** _________________

**Primary Vet:**
Name: _________________
Phone: _________________
Address: _________________
Hours: _________________

**24-Hour Emergency Clinic:**
Name: _________________
Phone: _________________
Address: _________________
Driving time: _____

**Poison Control:**
- ASPCA: (888) 426-4435
- Pet Poison Helpline: (855) 764-7661

**Backup Caretaker:**
Name: _________________
Phone: _________________

### 2. Medical Information Sheet

Keep updated copy in carrier and with emergency contacts:

- Cat's full name, age, breed
- Microchip number
- Current medications (name, dose, frequency)
- Known allergies
- Current health conditions
- Last vet visit date
- Vaccination dates
- Your contact information
- Insurance policy number (if applicable)

### 3. Emergency Kit

Assemble and keep accessible:

**First Aid Supplies:**
- Gauze pads and rolls
- Medical tape
- Scissors (blunt-tipped)
- Tweezers
- Digital thermometer
- Saline eye wash
- Styptic powder (for nail bleeding)
- Hydrogen peroxide (to induce vomiting ONLY if vet instructs)
- Disposable gloves
- Towels for restraint/warmth
- Emergency blanket

**Essential Items:**
- Secure cat carrier (always assembled)
- Extra collar with ID
- Recent photo of cat
- 3-day supply of food/medication
- Can opener
- Food/water bowls
- Litter pan and litter
- Cat's favorite toy or blanket

**Documents (waterproof container):**
- Medical records copy
- Vaccination records
- Medication list
- Emergency contact list
- Insurance information
- Vet records

### 4. Emergency Scenario Plans

**House Fire:**
- Carrier location
- Cat's likely hiding spots
- Rescue window stickers (show emergency responders)

**Natural Disaster:**
- Evacuation plan
- Pet-friendly shelter list
- Transport plan
- Meeting point if separated

**Sudden Illness/Injury:**
- Step-by-step: assess, stabilize, transport
- When to call ahead vs. go immediately
- How to safely transport injured cat

**If You're Incapacitated:**
- Who has keys?
- Where is medical info?
- Emergency caretaker instructions

### 5. CPR and Basic First Aid

Provide guidance on:
- Checking vital signs
- How to perform cat CPR (with disclaimer to get training)
- Heimlich for choking
- Controlling bleeding
- Handling fractures
- Treating burns
- Heatstroke response

**Important:** Include resources for formal pet first aid training.

## Output

- Printable emergency contact cards
- Medical information sheet template
- Emergency kit checklist
- Scenario-specific action plans
- Basic first aid quick reference guide
- Resources for formal training and certification
