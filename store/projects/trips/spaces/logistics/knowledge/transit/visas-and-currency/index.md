# Visa and currency advisory

The navigator writes one `knowledge_notes` row per trip covering entry requirements and money
matters for the destinations involved — the kind of thing a traveller needs to know weeks before
departure (does a visa need applying for in advance, is there a Schengen day-count to track) and
the kind of thing that's genuinely useful the day they land (is this a tap-to-pay country or a
cash one, what do ATMs here typically charge).

This is orientation, not legal or immigration advice, and every note the navigator writes must say
so plainly. Entry requirements change with visa-policy updates, bilateral agreements, and even the
traveller's specific passport and travel history in ways a general search can't fully capture —
`schengen-and-visas.md` covers the recurring patterns (visa-on-arrival norms, the Schengen 90/180
rule, e-visa/ETA schemes) framed explicitly as a starting point, always closing with "verify with
the relevant consulate or embassy before booking anything non-refundable." `currency-and-cash.md`
covers the practical money-handling side — cash-vs-card norms by region, typical ATM/exchange
traps, and tipping conventions — which carries less risk of being wrong in a consequential way but
still deserves a source citation rather than folk wisdom.

The navigator should write this note early enough to be useful — ideally as soon as destinations
are known, well before any transit leg's `bookByDate` — since visa processing (when required) can
take weeks, and finding out about an e-visa requirement three days before a flight is the kind of
failure this knowledge exists to prevent.
