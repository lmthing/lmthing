# Step 2: Weekly Health Check Routine

**Agent:** Vet Visit Planner

## Task

Create a simple weekly health examination routine.

## Instructions

Design a 5-10 minute weekly health check:

### Weekly Exam Checklist

**Eyes:**
- Clear and bright?
- No discharge or tearing?
- Pupils equal size?
- No cloudiness or color change?

**Ears:**
- Clean inside?
- No odor?
- No excessive wax or discharge?
- No redness or swelling?
- Cat doesn't shake head or scratch ears?

**Nose:**
- Clean and slightly moist?
- No discharge?
- Normal breathing?

**Mouth and Teeth:**
- Gums pink (not pale, white, or red)?
- Teeth clean without excessive tartar?
- No broken teeth?
- Breath reasonably fresh?
- No drooling?

**Coat and Skin:**
- Coat shiny and clean?
- No bald patches?
- No fleas, ticks, or excessive dandruff?
- Skin supple when gently tented?
- No lumps, bumps, or wounds?

**Body Condition:**
- Can you feel ribs with light pressure?
- Visible waist when viewed from above?
- No sudden weight gain or loss?

**Mobility:**
- Moving normally with no limping?
- Jumping up and down comfortably?
- No stiffness, especially after rest?

**Behavior Check:**
- Normal energy level?
- Eating and drinking normally?
- Litter box habits unchanged?
- Normal grooming behavior?
- Usual social interaction?
- Normal vocalization?

### How to Conduct the Exam

1. Choose a calm time when cat is relaxed
2. Use treats to create positive association
3. Be gentle and patient
4. Part fur to check skin
5. Lift lips gently to see gums/teeth
6. Feel along body for lumps
7. Observe movement and behavior
8. Record any concerns

## Output

A printable weekly health check form with checkboxes and space for notes. Include what's normal vs. concerning for each item.
