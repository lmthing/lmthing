# Recognising urgency without diagnosing

Most of what the clinic app surfaces — a flagged lipid panel, a mild recurring symptom, a slow
trend in a metric — is squarely "worth discussing at the next routine visit" territory. A smaller
set of patterns are different: they generally warrant *prompt* attention rather than waiting for a
routine follow-up. The app's job in these cases is narrow and specific: notice the pattern and say
"consider seeking care soon" — it is never the app's job to say what's wrong or how urgent on a
finer scale than that.

`red-flags.md` lists the kinds of symptom and value patterns that generally warrant prompt or
urgent care. `when-to-see-a-doctor.md` gives the calmer, more common decision guide — routine vs.
soon vs. urgent — that most flagged findings actually fall into. Both exist to keep the app's role
honest: it informs, it never triages definitively, and it never replaces a clinician's judgment
with its own.
