# Rondo Form

## Definition
Multi-part form with recurring main section (A) alternating with contrasting sections.

## Characteristics
- Main theme (A) returns multiple times
- Contrasting sections (B, C, etc.) between returns
- Creates sense of unity with variety
- Common structure: A-B-A-C-A or A-B-A-C-A-B-A

## Examples
- Classical rondo movements
- Some march forms
- Theme and variations endings

## Teaching Approach
- Extends understanding of repetition with contrast
- More complex than ternary
- Develops larger-scale form perception
- Common in classical period
