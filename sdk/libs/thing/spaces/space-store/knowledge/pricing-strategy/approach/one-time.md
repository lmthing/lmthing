# One-Time Purchase Pricing

Set a fixed price that buyers pay once to download the complete agent into their workspace. Simple, transparent, and easy for buyers to evaluate.

## Pricing Factors

Consider these when setting your price:

- **Complexity** — more agents, flows, and knowledge domains justify higher prices
- **Knowledge depth** — extensive, curated knowledge bases are valuable
- **Uniqueness** — if nothing similar exists, you can price higher
- **Target audience** — enterprise users tolerate higher prices than hobbyists
- **Effort invested** — account for research, testing, and iteration time

## Recommended Price Ranges

- **$5–$15** — Simple agents with basic knowledge, 1-2 flows
- **$15–$50** — Specialized agents with curated knowledge bases, 3+ flows
- **$50–$200** — Comprehensive multi-agent spaces with extensive flows and deep knowledge

## Best Practices

- Research competitor pricing for similar agents on the store
- Start slightly lower to build initial sales and reviews, then adjust
- Offer a free preview or demo to reduce purchase anxiety
- Include clear documentation — well-documented agents get fewer refunds
- Consider bundle pricing if you have multiple related agents
- Update the agent periodically — buyers appreciate ongoing maintenance

## Common Mistakes

- Pricing too low undervalues your work and signals low quality
- Pricing too high without demonstrating value reduces conversions
- Not having a preview makes buyers hesitant to commit
