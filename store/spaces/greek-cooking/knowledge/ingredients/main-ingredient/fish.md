# Fish (Ψάρι)

Fresh fish is the pride of coastal Greek cuisine, prepared simply to showcase quality.

## Common Greek Fish
- **Lavraki (Sea Bass)**: Most prized, grilled whole
- **Tsipoura (Sea Bream)**: Sweet, delicate flesh
- **Barbounia (Red Mullet)**: Small, pan-fried whole
- **Bakaliaros (Cod)**: Salted/fresh, fried with skordalia
- **Sardeles (Sardines)**: Grilled, fried, or marinated
- **Marides (Whitebait)**: Dusted and fried crispy

## Cooking Methods
- **Grilling**: Whole fish, olive oil, lemon
- **Plaki**: Baked with tomatoes and vegetables
- **Fried**: Dusted in flour, fried crispy
- **Marinated**: Raw or cured with lemon

## Cooking Tips
- Fresh fish should smell like the sea, not fishy
- Score flesh for even cooking
- Don't flip fish more than once
- Let rest 2-3 minutes before serving
- Always have lemon wedges available

## The Greek Way
Whole fish, simply grilled, is considered the finest meal. Don't complicate perfection.
