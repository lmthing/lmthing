# Ladera (Λαδερά)

Unique Greek technique: vegetables slow-cooked in olive oil until meltingly tender.

## Philosophy
- Olive oil is a primary ingredient, not a cooking fat
- Vegetables cook until very soft (not crisp-tender)
- Served at room temperature
- Perfect for fasting periods
- Improves overnight

## Technique
1. Sauté aromatics in generous olive oil
2. Add vegetables
3. Add tomatoes (usually)
4. Add water to almost cover
5. Simmer uncovered until oil rises
6. Cool to room temperature

## Classic Ladera Dishes
- Fasolakia (green beans)
- Bamies (okra)
- Arakas (peas)
- Gigantes plaki (giant beans)
- Imam baildi (stuffed eggplant)
- Briam (mixed vegetables)
- Aginares ala polita (artichokes)

## How Much Oil?
More than you think. Traditionally, the oil should "swim" on top when done. At least 1/2 cup for a pot.

## Serving
- Room temperature (not cold, not hot)
- Fresh bread for sopping
- Feta cheese on the side
- Squeeze of lemon optional
