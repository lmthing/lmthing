# Light and orientation

## Compass claims are checkable against other stated facts

A listing claiming "bright, south-facing living room" (in the northern hemisphere, the orientation
that gets the most direct sun) is a specific, falsifiable-from-text claim — cross-check it against
anything else in the text that implies orientation: a caption mentioning a specific street-facing or
courtyard-facing view, a floor plan's compass indicator if described, or a neighboring building
described as "opposite" or "facing" a particular direction. When nothing contradicts the claim, it's
not evidence FOR it either — the absence of a contradiction is not confirmation, so a lone unverified
orientation claim shouldn't get restated as an established fact in your `body`.

## "Bright" is a marketing word until something backs it

"Bright" and "sunny" are among the most overused, least-verified adjectives in listing copy — treat a
bare "bright" claim as marketing language, not evidence, unless something concrete supports it: a
caption describing floor-to-ceiling windows, a stated large window count, a top-floor/corner-unit
position (more exposure than a mid-block, mid-floor unit), or an explicit orientation claim that
itself has some support. A listing that says "bright" with photos captioned only "living room,"
"bedroom," "kitchen" (no descriptive detail at all) has given you nothing to forensically confirm or
contradict — say so plainly rather than either endorsing or debunking the claim.

## Courtyard, interior-facing, and light wells are the honest downgrade

A caption or description mentioning a "courtyard view," "interior-facing," or a light well/airshaft
is one of the more reliable NEGATIVE light signals available from text alone — these units
structurally get materially less direct light than a street-facing or unobstructed-view unit
regardless of what the headline description claims. When this shows up alongside a "bright" claim in
the same listing, that's exactly the kind of `mismatch` worth flagging, phrased as a question the
photos/viewing should settle: "the description calls this unit bright, but photo 6's caption
mentions a courtyard view — worth confirming actual daylight at a viewing."
