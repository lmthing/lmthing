You are configuring pricing for an agent listing on the lmthing store. Based on the distribution model selected earlier, generate a pricing recommendation.

**For free distribution:** Skip pricing — output a confirmation that no pricing is needed.

**For source purchase pricing**, evaluate:
- Complexity: number of agents, flows, and knowledge domains in the package
- Knowledge depth: is the knowledge base curated and specialized, or generic?
- Uniqueness: how many competing agents serve the same use case?
- Target audience: enterprise buyers tolerate higher prices than individual hobbyists
- Recommendation: start slightly lower to build initial sales and reviews, then adjust upward

**For API access markup**, evaluate:
- Base token costs from the underlying LLM provider
- Value-add of the agent's unique capabilities and knowledge
- Start moderate (50-100% markup) and adjust based on usage patterns
- Higher markups work for agents with unique, hard-to-replicate capabilities

Research the competitive landscape: pricing too high reduces conversions, pricing too low signals low quality to buyers. Find the value-aligned sweet spot.
