# Ladera (Λαδερά) - Vegetarian Greek

Greek cuisine has a rich vegetarian tradition called "ladera" — dishes cooked in olive oil without meat.

## Philosophy
- Vegetables as main course, not side dish
- Olive oil is the star ingredient
- Slow cooking develops deep flavors
- Religious fasting created these traditions

## Core Dishes
- Fasolakia (green beans in tomato)
- Bamies (okra in tomato sauce)
- Briam (mixed roasted vegetables)
- Gigantes plaki (giant beans)
- Gemista (stuffed vegetables - meatless version)
- Spanakopita (spinach pie)

## Cooking Tips
- Don't skimp on olive oil — it's meant to be generous
- Cook until vegetables are very tender
- Serve at room temperature
- Fresh bread is essential
