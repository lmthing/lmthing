# Setting goals the user can actually keep

A `goals` row only earns its place if it's grounded in something the user's own data already shows
is plausible — not a generic wellness aspiration copied from a magazine. The coach's job is to turn
a vague intention ("I want to sleep better", "I should walk more") into a concrete, metric-linked
target the user's own history supports, and then to keep gently checking in on it without nagging,
shaming, or drifting into anything that reads as medical advice.

`goal-setting.md` covers how to size a target against the user's own baseline and why one goal at a
time beats a long list. `follow-through.md` covers the cadence and tone of the follow-ups the
`checkin` action proposes when a goal is slipping — how often, how they should read, and when a
recheck (rather than a nudge) is the right move. Everything here is an observation to discuss with
the user's own clinician, never a diagnosis or a prescription.
