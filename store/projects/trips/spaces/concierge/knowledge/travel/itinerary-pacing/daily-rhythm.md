# Daily rhythm

## How much to schedule per day

As a working default: **one anchor activity in the morning, one in the afternoon, and unstructured
time around meals** — not four or five timed attractions crammed into a single day. A "big" museum
or landmark, once genuinely engaged with (not just photographed from outside), tends to consume
2-4 hours including queueing and getting there; two of those plus meals already fills a normal day.
When the research report flags several must-dos for one destination, that's a signal the
destination needs *more days*, not that the days need to hold more items — flag this tension
rather than silently cramming.

## Meal cadence

Meals are not filler between activities, they're `itinerary_items` in their own right (`kind:
'meal'`) and deserve their own scheduled slot with a rough time window, especially for a trip where
food is a stated priority. A reasonable default is breakfast (often informal/unscheduled, can be
omitted as an explicit item), lunch around midday with a real duration (an hour-plus for a sit-down
meal, not a 15-minute placeholder), and dinner in the evening. Where the research flagged a specific
food recommendation, make sure it lands as its own item near a mealtime rather than getting
mentioned only in passing notes on an unrelated activity.

## Buffer time

Always leave gaps — 30-60 minutes between scheduled items minimum inside a city, more when the
research or transit notes suggest congestion, long walks, or unpredictable queues. A day scheduled
back-to-back with zero slack means any single delay (a queue, a wrong turn, a shop that was more
interesting than expected) cascades into missing the next thing, which cascades into missing the
one after. Buffer isn't wasted time in the plan — it's what makes the plan survivable.

## Arrival and departure days

The day the traveller arrives at a new destination is not a normal day — travel fatigue, luggage,
check-in logistics, and simply getting oriented eat most of it. Schedule arrival days light: one
easy, low-commitment activity near the lodging at most, or none at all beyond settling in and a
relaxed dinner. The same applies in reverse to departure days, especially with an early transit
leg the next morning — don't schedule a full day of activities immediately before an early
departure.

## Morning vs. evening

Match activity type to time of day using what the research turned up about crowds and light:
outdoor/scenic spots and popular landmarks are usually best early (softer light, fewer crowds, often
literally listed as "quieter before 10am" in traveller reports); markets and food-focused
neighborhoods often peak midday; nightlife-adjacent and view-from-above activities (rooftop bars,
sunset viewpoints) belong in the evening slot. When the research report is silent on timing, default
to what generally reduces crowding rather than a default of "sightseeing in the morning" applied
mechanically.
