# Step 1: Establish Health Baseline

**Agent:** Vet Visit Planner

## Task

Document your cat's normal health parameters to identify changes early.

## Inputs

- Cat's age and breed
- Current health conditions
- Recent vet records
- Current weight
- Normal behavior patterns

## Instructions

Create a baseline health profile:

### 1. Physical Measurements
- Current weight
- Body condition score (1-9 scale)
- Normal temperature range
- Normal heart rate range
- Normal respiratory rate

### 2. Normal Behaviors
- Typical activity level
- Sleep patterns
- Appetite (amount and frequency)
- Water consumption
- Litter box habits (frequency, appearance)
- Grooming behavior
- Vocalization patterns
- Social interaction level

### 3. Physical Appearance Normals
- Coat condition and quality
- Eye appearance
- Ear cleanliness
- Teeth and gum condition
- Nail length
- Skin condition

### 4. Known Conditions
- Chronic health issues
- Medications and dosages
- Previous injuries or surgeries
- Allergies or sensitivities
- Breed-specific concerns

## Output

A "Know Your Cat" baseline document that will be used to spot deviations from normal. This should be updated every 6 months or after health changes.
